About Me

I'm Mar Tejedor Ninou, a PhD researcher at the Technical University of Catalunya (UPC) and the Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC), where I work on heterogeneous HPC systems and distributed runtime models for emerging architectures under the supervision of Rosa M. Badia. My research sits at the intersection of high-performance computing and quantum computing.

I hold an MSc in Computer Science from Georgia Tech (Machine Learning & Computing Systems) and an MSc in Quantum Science and Technology from the University of Barcelona. My PhD is supported by the Doctoral INPhINIT Retaining Fellowship from La Caixa Foundation — I was the sole Computer Science/Engineering awardee in Spain in 2025. I am currently doing a research internship at NVIDIA in Zurich, focusing on multi-GPU scheduling and distributed runtime optimization.
